    Methodology

    Each Monday, an automated pipeline gathers tenure data and macroeconomic indicators for every tracked leader, then asks Claude (with web search enabled) to verify current polling, review the past seven days of news, and place the leader on the doom-loop circle. The angle on the circle reflects where the leader sits in the four-phase cycle. The doom score is normalized per phase so that a higher score consistently means "deeper into the doom loop" — a leader in Phase III will always score higher than a comparable one in Phase I. Both are produced by a language model and should be read as commentary, not as data in a scientific sense.
